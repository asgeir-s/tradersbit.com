How does Autotrader work?

Autotrader will trade the stream's incoming signals, on the subscriber's behalf.

For now, autotrader only does margin trading. Therefore, the money you want autotrader to trade with must be in your "Trading Wallet" at Bitfinex.

All trades executed by Autotrader are market orders. A market order is a buy or a sell order to be executed immediately at current market prices. This means that you should not expect to get the exact same price as recorded in the trading signal, because of slippage, latency, etc. Read about slippage on Investopedia.

On the first signal, autotrader will close any open BTC/USD margin position before executing the signaled order. When executing an order, the amount autotrader will use to buy or sell short is your specified "Percentage to Trade" of "Tradable Balance" in your "Trading Wallet". If you specified 100% "Percentage to Trade", it will use 99.9%, this is done to make sure you have the funds to cover the order at the order time.

There is an average latency of approximately 2 seconds from a signal is published by the publisher, until the order is executed at the exchange. Therefore, this should not be thought of as a high-frequency trading solution.

How do I stop autotrader before the subscription e

Sign into Bitfinex, go to API key Management, then revoke the API key you entered when subscribing with autotrader.

How do TradersBit simulate trades?

When simulating trades (LONG, SHORT, CLOSE), we use the last price and 0.02% fee (takers fee). Each signal will simulate a trade using all assets available.

What does LONG, SHORT and CLOSE mean?

SHORT, LONG, and CLOSE are the signalks you can send to your streams. A SHORT or LONG signal is always followed by a CLOSE signal. If you go directly from SHORT to LONG or LONG to SHORT, a CLOSE signal is automatically generated for you.

If you send a LONG signal, you will make a profit if the price of the Bitcoin goes up. You profit if the price of the Bitcoin is higher when you CLOSE your trade than it was when you sent the LONG signal. Read about LONGING on Investopedia.

If you send a SHORT signal, you will make a profit if the price of the Bitcoin goes down. You profit if the price of the Bitcoin is lower when you CLOSE your trade than it was when you sent the SHORT signal. Read about SHORTING on Investopedia..

A CLOSE signal closes your current position.
