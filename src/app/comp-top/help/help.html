How it works

This is a marketplace for streams of Bitcoin trading signals.

Publishers create streams, send trading signals to streams and sell subscriptions to streams. Statistics for each stream’s performance is computed and displayed in the marketplace. The statistics include trading fees (unless explicitly stated otherwise).

Subscribers can browse and sort streams based on average monthly profit, average trade and other indicators. When subscribing to a stream, the subscriber can get signals sent by email or the trade can be executed automatically on the subscriber’s behalf (coming soon).

The publisher decides the subscription fee (monthly fee) and when a subscription is sold, the publisher immediately receives 70% of the subscription fee.

Upon reception of a trading signal, we simulate the trade (including trading fees). This way there is now way for the publishers to manipulate statistics.

There is no social interaction on this marketplace, because the statistics and trading signals speak for them self. This way subscribers will not be easily scammed. A subscriber should not know or care whether signals are sent manually by a kid, an MBA graduate or automatically generated by a trading boot (algorithm trading).

Signals can be published through our website, mobile app or API.

Our API makes it possible to write trading systems in any programming language and easily integrate it with existing trading systems (bots).

TradersBit will also host trading competitions, for instance most profitable stream last 30 days, etc. Where all rewards are in Bitcoin.

Signals

Signals you can send are SHORT, LONG and CLOSE. A SHORT or LONG signal is always followed by a CLOSE signal. If you go directly from SHORT to LONG or LONG to SHORT a CLOSE signal is automatically generated for you.

If you send a LONG signal you will make a profit if the price of Bitcoin goes up. You profit if the price of Bitcoin is higher when you CLOSE your trade, then it was when you sent the LONG signal. Investopedia on longing.

If you send a SHORT signal, you will make a profit if the price of Bitcoin goes down. You profit if the price of Bitcoin is lower when you CLOSE your trade, then it was when you sent the SHORT signal. Investopedia on shorting.

A CLOSE signal closes your current position.

Each signal will simulate a trade using all assets available.

API

API for sending trading signals.

              
POST: 
https://api.tradersbit.com/streams/'stream id'/signal
Headers
  • Content-Type: application/json
  • x-authorization: apikey 'streams apikey'
Api keys can be generated in the My Streams view (under Publisher). Click on the three-dot icon on one of you streams (icon on the far right of you streams on the My Streams page).
Body
The body of the request should be -1 (SHORT), 0 (CLOSE) or 1 (LONG).

Example
                
POST: 
https://api.tradersbit.com/streams/4b0c2989-ca71-46c5-a138-c4777a41e13e/signal
